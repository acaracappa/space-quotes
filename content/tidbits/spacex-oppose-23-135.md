EchoStar asked the FCC to reverse a waiver the agency's bureaus had granted for SpaceX's
satellite-to-phone service. SpaceX notes that EchoStar was the only party to seek review, and
that it did so without offering any technical analysis of its own. SpaceX's April 23, 2025 reply
is not subtle: EchoStar's argument "ignores the very purpose of a waiver—which is to authorize
the deviation from a rule in appropriate circumstances," and the company asks the Commission to
deny the appeal and "remove any cloud over SpaceX's ongoing deployment."

The waiver underpins the direct-to-cell service SpaceX is building with T-Mobile. The dispute is
one of 125 filings in docket 23-135.

> "EchoStar clearly wants to stymie competition by hamstringing SpaceX's SCS system, but its arguments against the waiver are frivolous"
> — SpaceX, opposition filing, FCC docket 23-135

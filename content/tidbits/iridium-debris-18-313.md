Iridium has a specific fix in mind for satellites that blow up. In a June 24, 2024 letter to the
FCC's orbital-debris docket, the operator argued the Commission "should adopt a firm threshold
for accidental explosions in accordance with NASA Standards." A rule on paper is not enough, it
added: the threshold "must be applied evenly across all operators" and clearly defined "so that
it may be calculated and enforced." Iridium asked the FCC to seek comment on exactly how.

The proceeding, IB Docket 18-313 ("Mitigation of Orbital Debris in the New Space Age"), has
drawn 339 filings, including 159 comments and 125 ex parte notices.

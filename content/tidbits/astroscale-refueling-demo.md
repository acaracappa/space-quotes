Astroscale wants to refuel satellites in orbit, and its FCC license application spells out the
hardware that would do it. The spacecraft, called APS-R (Astroscale Prototype Servicer for
Refueling), "will be the first spacecraft to perform cooperative in-space refueling
demonstrations with client vehicles in supersynchronous geostationary orbit," the December 23,
2024 filing says. In two planned demonstrations it would both deliver fuel to a prepared client
vehicle, CV-1, and take on fuel itself from a separate depot, using docking-and-transfer
interfaces supplied by Orbit Fab.

The propellant is hydrazine, carried in a 30 kg tank, and the program is funded: APS-R is under
construction on a $25.5 million cost-share contract with the U.S. Space Force's Space Systems
Command, with launch expected in mid-2026.

> "the first spacecraft to perform cooperative in-space refueling demonstrations with client vehicles in supersynchronous geostationary orbit"
> — Astroscale, FCC license application
